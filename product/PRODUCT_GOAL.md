# Product Goal

Ross Workout Coach is not trying to be the most powerful workout app.

It is trying to be the easiest workout app to use while actually working out.

The app should help someone answer three questions quickly:

1. What am I doing today?
2. What do I do next?
3. What can I do if this exercise, equipment, time, or plan does not work for me?

Core workout use must work locally. A user should be able to open the app, continue the current plan, complete exercises, skip or adjust when needed, and keep moving without sync, AI, an account, or backend setup.

AI, Google Sheets sync, generated plans, and remote alternatives may enhance the app, but they must not be required for the core workout experience.

Flexibility belongs inside the workout flow. If a machine is unavailable, time is short, a movement hurts, or the plan does not fit the day, the app should offer a simple next-best action instead of making the user leave the workout or repair the plan manually.

Every feature should make it easier to complete today's workout. If a request makes the app more powerful but less obvious, future contributors should push back before coding and propose a simpler option.

The app should feel simple, obvious, fast, flexible, forgiving, calm, and non-rigid.

The app should not feel cluttered, fragile, over-explained, spreadsheet-like, medical-dashboard-like, dependent on AI/backend setup, or like one screen was patched without considering the full flow.
